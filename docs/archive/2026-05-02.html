CISA Adds One Known Exploited Vulnerability to Catalog

The addition of CVE-2026-31431 to the KEV Catalog signals an urgent need for immediate remediation to prevent potential exploitation.

Who should care: Federal Civilian Executive Branch agencies and organizations using Linux systems should prioritize addressing this vulnerability.
Attack path: An attacker can exploit this vulnerability to gain unauthorized access to system resources, potentially leading to further compromise of the network.
Why this matters now: KEV status shortens the acceptable patch window.
In plain English: CISA has identified a new critical vulnerability in the Linux Kernel that is actively being exploited.
cve linux federal
  • CISA has added CVE-2026-31431, a vulnerability in the Linux Kernel, to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities Catalog due to evidence of active exploitation. This vulnerability involves incorrect resource transfer between spheres, making it a frequent target for attackers.
  • The exploitation of this vulnerability poses significant risks, particularly to federal agencies, as it can lead to unauthorized access and control over affected systems. Timely remediation is crucial to protect networks from potential cyberattacks.
  • An attacker can exploit this vulnerability to gain unauthorized access to system resources, potentially leading to further compromise of the network.
  • Watch next: Monitor for updates from CISA regarding additional vulnerabilities added to the KEV Catalog and any recommended remediation timelines.
Key terms

  • KEV - Known Exploited Vulnerabilities, a catalog of vulnerabilities that are actively being exploited in the wild.

30,000 Facebook Accounts Hacked via Google AppSheet Phishing Campaign

The compromise of 30,000 Facebook accounts through a Google AppSheet phishing campaign poses significant risks to user privacy and security.

Who should care: Social media teams, security teams, and users of Facebook accounts are most exposed.
Attack path: Attackers leverage Google AppSheet to create convincing phishing emails that trick users into revealing their Facebook credentials.
Why this matters now: Useful context for teams tracking where attacker pressure is building.
In plain English: A phishing campaign using Google AppSheet has led to the hacking of 30,000 Facebook accounts.
phishing facebook google
  • A Vietnamese-linked operation has been identified using Google AppSheet to distribute phishing emails aimed at compromising Facebook accounts. This campaign, named AccountDumpling, has resulted in the theft of approximately 30,000 accounts, which are being sold through an illicit storefront.
  • The stolen Facebook accounts can be used for various malicious activities, including identity theft, spreading misinformation, or further phishing attacks. The use of a legitimate platform like Google AppSheet for phishing increases the likelihood of user engagement and success of the attack.
  • Attackers leverage Google AppSheet to create convincing phishing emails that trick users into revealing their Facebook credentials.
  • Watch next: Monitor for any updates from Facebook regarding account recovery measures and potential impacts on user security.
Key terms
  • Phishing - a method of trying to gather personal information using deceptive emails and websites.
  • Google AppSheet - a no-code development platform that allows users to create applications without programming.
Defender focus
  • Implement email filtering to detect and block phishing attempts using Google AppSheet.
  • Monitor for unusual login attempts on Facebook accounts linked to the compromised credentials.
  • Educate users on recognizing phishing emails and the importance of two-factor authentication.
Do now
  • Enhance email security measures to filter out phishing attempts.
  • Alert users to change their Facebook passwords immediately.
  • Investigate any suspicious activity related to compromised accounts.
Watch next: Monitor for any updates from Facebook regarding account recovery measures and potential impacts on user security.
