Supply chain attack at CPUID pushes malware with CPU-Z/HWMonitor

The recent supply chain attack on CPUID compromises the integrity of widely used system monitoring tools, potentially leading to widespread malware infections.

Who should care: IT security teams and users of CPU-Z and HWMonitor should be particularly vigilant.
Attack path: Attackers exploited a vulnerability in the CPUID API to serve malicious files instead of legitimate software. Users who downloaded the compromised tools may unknowingly execute malware on their systems.
Why this matters now: Useful context for teams tracking where attacker pressure is building.
In plain English: Malicious executables were served through official download links for CPU-Z and HWMonitor due to a supply chain compromise.
supply chain malware cpu z hwmonitor
  • Hackers accessed an API for the CPUID project and altered the download links on the official website to distribute malware disguised as legitimate software. This incident affects users who downloaded CPU-Z and HWMonitor during the compromise period.
  • The malware could allow attackers to gain unauthorized access to systems, steal sensitive information, or deploy additional malicious payloads. Given the popularity of these tools, a significant number of users may be at risk, amplifying the potential impact.
  • Attackers exploited a vulnerability in the CPUID API to serve malicious files instead of legitimate software. Users who downloaded the compromised tools may unknowingly execute malware on their systems.
  • Watch next: Monitor for updates from CPUID regarding the incident and any patches or mitigations they release.
Key terms
  • Supply Chain Attack - a type of cyber attack where the attacker infiltrates a system through an outside partner or service provider.
  • Malware - malicious software designed to harm, exploit, or otherwise compromise a computer system.
Defender focus
  • Verify the integrity of software installations for CPU-Z and HWMonitor.
  • Implement endpoint detection to identify any unusual behavior from these applications.
  • Educate users on the risks of downloading software from compromised sources.
Do now
  • Immediately check for and remove any installations of CPU-Z and HWMonitor downloaded during the compromise.
  • Update antivirus definitions and run a full system scan on affected machines.
  • Stay informed about CPUID's response and any further developments related to this incident.
