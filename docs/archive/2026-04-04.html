China-Linked TA416 Targets European Governments with PlugX and OAuth-Based Phishing

European governments are facing heightened cyber threats from a China-linked group, risking sensitive diplomatic information.

Who should care: Government cybersecurity teams and diplomatic IT departments are most exposed to these threats.
Attack path: Attackers leverage OAuth-based phishing to gain initial access, tricking users into providing credentials that allow for further exploitation.
Why this matters now: Sector exposure makes this broader than a one-off incident.
In plain English: TA416 is actively targeting European government entities using advanced phishing techniques.
phishing malware government
  • Since mid-2025, the China-aligned threat actor TA416 has been targeting European government and diplomatic organizations. This follows a two-year period of reduced activity in the region. The group employs PlugX malware and OAuth-based phishing tactics to compromise systems.
  • The targeting of government entities poses significant risks to national security and diplomatic relations. Successful breaches could lead to the exposure of sensitive information and undermine public trust in governmental institutions.
