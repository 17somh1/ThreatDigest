Iranian-Affiliated Cyber Actors Exploit Programmable Logic Controllers Across US Critical Infrastructure

Iranian-affiliated cyber actors are actively exploiting critical infrastructure PLCs, posing a significant risk of operational disruption and financial loss.

Who should care: Defensive cybersecurity analysts, OT cybersecurity engineers, and organizations operating in critical infrastructure sectors should be particularly vigilant.
Attack path: Attackers gain access to PLCs through internet exposure and exploit vulnerabilities using specialized software, allowing them to manipulate operational data.
Why this matters now: KEV status shortens the acceptable patch window. A live patch or vendor fix gives defenders something concrete to validate.
In plain English: U.S. critical infrastructure is under threat from Iranian cyber actors targeting PLCs, necessitating immediate security reviews.
cybersecurity plc iran
  • Iran-affiliated advanced persistent threat (APT) actors are exploiting internet-facing programmable logic controllers (PLCs) from Rockwell Automation/Allen-Bradley, causing disruptions across multiple U.S. critical infrastructure sectors. This exploitation involves malicious interactions with project files and data manipulation on human machine interface (HMI) and supervisory control and data acquisition (SCADA) displays.
  • The ongoing exploitation of PLCs can lead to severe operational disruptions and financial losses for organizations in critical sectors such as government services, water systems, and energy. Given the widespread use of these devices, the potential for further targeting of other brands increases the urgency for organizations to secure their systems.
  • Attackers gain access to PLCs through internet exposure and exploit vulnerabilities using specialized software, allowing them to manipulate operational data.
  • Watch next: Monitor for updates from CISA and Rockwell Automation regarding new IOCs and recommended mitigations.
Key terms
  • Programmable Logic Controller (PLC) - a digital computer used for automation of industrial processes.
  • Human Machine Interface (HMI) - a user interface that connects operators to the controller for monitoring and control.
Defender focus
  • Review logs for suspicious traffic on ports 44818, 2222, 102, and 502.
  • Implement network segmentation to isolate PLCs from direct internet access.
  • Ensure all PLCs are updated with the latest security patches and configurations.
Do now
  • Immediately disconnect PLCs from the internet to prevent unauthorized access.
  • Review and apply CISA's recommended mitigations for PLC security.
  • Conduct a thorough audit of network traffic to identify any signs of compromise.
