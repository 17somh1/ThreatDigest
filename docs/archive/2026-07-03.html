Ransomware Groups Turn to Citrix Bleed 2, BYOVD, and Supply Chain Credentials

Who should care: IT security teams managing Citrix environments and organizations using RMM tools should be particularly vigilant.
Attack path: Attackers gain leverage by exploiting the Citrix Bleed 2 vulnerability to access systems, then use stolen credentials to move laterally within the network.
Why this matters now: Useful context for teams tracking where attacker pressure is building.
In plain English: Anubis ransomware actors are exploiting Citrix Bleed 2 to infiltrate networks, which could lead to significant data breaches.
ransomware cve cloud
  • Threat actors from the Anubis ransomware operation have been observed exploiting the Citrix Bleed 2 vulnerability (CVE-2025-5777) to gain initial access to networks. They utilize legitimate Remote Management and Monitoring (RMM) tools alongside credential theft to facilitate lateral movement within compromised environments.
  • The exploitation of Citrix Bleed 2 can lead to unauthorized access to sensitive data and systems, increasing the risk of data breaches and operational disruptions. Organizations that rely on Citrix products without proper patching or monitoring are particularly vulnerable to these attacks.
