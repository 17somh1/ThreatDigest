Laser Attack Resets Tangem Wallet Passwords on Cards That Can't Be Patched

A laser attack can compromise Tangem wallet cards, allowing unauthorized access to cryptocurrency funds, which poses a significant risk to users.

Who should care: Tangem wallet users and security teams managing cryptocurrency assets should be particularly vigilant.
Attack path: An attacker can leverage a laser to reset the wallet's password, gaining full control over the funds stored on the card.
Why this matters now: A live patch or vendor fix gives defenders something concrete to validate.
In plain English: Researchers demonstrated that a laser can reset the password on Tangem wallet cards, enabling theft of cryptocurrency.
crypto hardware wallet vulnerability
  • Researchers from Ledger's Donjon security team revealed that a precisely timed laser pulse can reset the password of Tangem crypto wallet cards. This attack allows the attacker to set a new password without needing the old one or a backup card, effectively giving them control over the wallet.
  • This vulnerability means that Tangem wallet users are at risk of losing their cryptocurrency without any means of recovery. As the attack requires specialized equipment and knowledge, it is not widespread, but it highlights a critical flaw in the security of hardware wallets.
